Two ways to type.

Both games share the same pedagogy — spaced repetition, motor-stage scaffolding, psychoacoustic feedback — but offer very different feels.

📚 TYPE LIBRARY
Constructive

Catch words. Build your library.

Words drift down from above. Type them to catch each book and shelve it. Twelve levels carry you from individual keys to full sentences and grammar correction. Every level completed seals a new shelf — your library grows as you do.

🚀 TYPE COMMAND
Combat

Type to fire. Words are your weapons.

Enemies descend in formation. Type their names to destroy them before they reach your pilot. The same levels and vocabulary as Type Library — but with urgency, combos, and a shield to protect. For learners who like pressure.

Built on real learning science.

Spaced repetition

Words you miss come back sooner. Words you know fade to the background. Your practice time goes exactly where it's needed.

Motor stage awareness

The Fitts & Posner model tracks whether you're in the cognitive, associative, or autonomous stage — and adjusts difficulty accordingly.

Psychoacoustic feedback

Every keystroke plays a note proportional to its position in the word. Mistakes fall gently away. Progress rings up a harmonic scale. Your fingers learn to listen.

No punishment, only progress

Missed words drift off — they don't end your run. Errors give you a directional cue, not a harsh alarm. The game stays constructive even when you struggle.

Submission criteria

What makes a good addition?

We're looking for games and tools that respect the player's time and intelligence. These are the questions we ask.

Does it run locally?

Open the HTML file — does it work immediately, with no internet required after the first load?

Does it teach or train?

Typing, logic, memory, music, language, math, code — anything where practice builds genuine capability.

Is it accessible?

Playable by beginners. Clear enough that no manual is needed. Forgiving of mistakes.

Is it respectful?

No dark patterns, no artificial difficulty spikes, no shame mechanics. Reward progress, not punishment.

Is the code clean?

We'll read it. Readable, commented, no obfuscation. Others may build on it.

Is it original?

Your own work, or a clearly licensed adaptation. Not a port of a commercial game.
